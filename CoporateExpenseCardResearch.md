Corporate expense cards are payment cards issued by a company to employees to pay for business-related expenses directly, instead of employees using personal money and requesting reimbursement.

## What they actually are

A corporate expense card is typically a credit or charge card tied to a company account. The company, not the employee, is financially responsible for the spending. Providers include companies like American Express, Brex, and Ramp.

## How they work in practice

* Company issues cards to employees or teams
* Each card has predefined limits and rules
* Employees spend directly on business needs
* Transactions are tracked in real time
* Finance teams review, categorize, and approve expenses
* Accounting systems sync automatically

This replaces the traditional “pay first, get reimbursed later” model.

## Core features (modern systems)

Modern corporate cards are not just cards. They are full expense management systems.

**1. Spend controls**

* Per-user or per-team limits
* Category restrictions (e.g. no personal shopping)
* Merchant blocking
* Time-based rules

**2. Real-time visibility**

* Every transaction is logged instantly
* Finance teams see who spent what, where, and why

**3. Receipt & compliance automation**

* Employees upload receipts or use auto-capture
* Policies enforced automatically
* Missing receipts flagged

**4. Accounting integration**

* Syncs with tools like QuickBooks or Xero
* Auto-categorization reduces manual bookkeeping

**5. Virtual cards**

* Generate unique cards per vendor or subscription
* Helps prevent fraud and control recurring payments

## Types of corporate cards

**Corporate liability cards**

* Company pays the bill
* Most common for startups and tech companies

**Individual liability cards**

* Employee is responsible initially, then reimbursed
* Still used in some traditional companies

**Prepaid / debit-style cards**

* Spend only what is preloaded
* Useful for strict budget control

## Why companies use them

* Eliminate reimbursements
* Reduce fraud and misuse
* Gain real-time financial visibility
* Enforce spending policies automatically
* Simplify accounting workflows


## The gap current systems still have:

* Limited programmability
* Weak trustless collaboration
* Centralized control
